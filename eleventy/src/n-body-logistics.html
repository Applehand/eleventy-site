N-body Logistics

N-body Logistics is a strategic 2D simulation where players, as Chief Corporate Officers, oversee an interplanetary conglomerate, optimizing orbital logistics, automating complex production chains, and navigating dynamic markets and corporate politics across evolving star systems, with character and corporate legacies persisting through each new game cycle.

The Gameplay Loop

The game is a blend of high-level strategic planning and real-time operational management. The core loop is structured around quarterly and yearly cycles, with distinct phases for decision-making.

Quarterly Strategic Decisions

At the end of each quarter, the game pauses for a strategic review. This is where you make high-level decisions that will shape the company's direction for the next quarter.

  • Budget Allocation: Allocate funds to each division (Extraction, Manufacturing, Logistics, etc.).
  • Performance Review: Analyze reports from each division and set new performance targets.
  • Contract Tenders: Accept or decline major contracts from governments and other corporations.
  • Shareholder Resolutions: Vote on proposals from shareholder factions.

Real-time Gameplay

Between quarters, the game runs in real-time (with adjustable speed). This is where you manage the day-to-day operations of your corporation.

  • Logistics Management: Design and manage trade routes, and respond to supply chain disruptions.
  • Factory Automation: Design and optimize production lines and supply chains.
  • Orbital Maneuvers: Adjust ship trajectories and manage fleet movements.
  • Crisis Management: Respond to pirate attacks, equipment failures, and other unexpected events.

Market & Corporate Dynamics

Navigate the complexities of shareholder expectations and define your corporate identity. Your strategy will be shaped by competing internal demands and your chosen niche in the interplanetary market.

Business Model Specialization

While an integrated conglomerate is possible, specialization offers strategic advantages. Focus your R&D and capital to dominate a specific market sector.

Shareholder Faction Interests

Balancing the conflicting demands of your shareholders is key to maintaining a high stock price and company reputation. Each decision has political consequences.

Solar Politics & Governmental Influence

Beyond corporate rivalries, the solar system is a tapestry of diverse governmental entities, each with their own laws, tenets, and long-standing legacies. Your corporation's interactions with these powers will profoundly shape your trajectory and the geopolitical landscape.

Government Contracts & Allegiance

Accepting contracts from various governments is not merely a financial transaction; it's an act of allegiance. Fulfilling these contracts can grant you exclusive resource rights, military protection, or favorable trade agreements, but may also alienate rival factions.

  • Exclusive Rights: Secure monopolies on valuable resources or trade routes within a government's sphere of influence.
  • Military Support: Gain access to governmental fleets for protection against pirates or corporate rivals.
  • Trade Privileges: Benefit from reduced tariffs, expedited customs, or preferential market access.
  • Reputational Impact: Your standing with one government can affect your reputation with others, potentially opening new opportunities or closing existing ones.

Laws, Tenets, and Legacies

Each government operates under a unique set of laws and guiding principles. Understanding and adapting to these is crucial for long-term success. Your actions will contribute to their ongoing legacies, and in turn, influence the solar system's future.

  • Regulatory Compliance: Adhere to varying environmental, labor, and trade regulations across different jurisdictions.
  • Ideological Alignment: Aligning with governments that share your corporate values can lead to deeper partnerships and mutual growth.
  • Historical Impact: Your corporation's history of cooperation or conflict with governments will be remembered, affecting future diplomatic and economic interactions.
  • Shaping the System: Through your influence and resource allocation, you can subtly (or overtly) shift the balance of power and the prevailing ideologies within the solar system.

The Annual Operations Cycle

The game progresses through a series of narrative-driven quarterly reports. These are not just spreadsheets; they are interactive events where you must make critical decisions, respond to challenges, and navigate the complex political landscape of your corporation.

The Quarterly Report: A Test of Leadership

Each quarter, you will be presented with a comprehensive report detailing the performance of each division. This is your opportunity to shape the future of the company. You will be faced with a variety of situations, such as:

  • A critical equipment failure in the Manufacturing division has led to a production shortfall. Do you authorize emergency repairs, cutting into your profits, or risk missing a contractual deadline?
  • The R&D division has made a breakthrough in automation, but it will require a significant investment to implement. Do you approve the funding, hoping for long-term gains, or focus on short-term profitability?
  • A rival corporation is attempting a hostile takeover of a key supplier. Do you intervene, potentially sparking a corporate war, or allow the acquisition to proceed and risk supply chain disruptions?
  • The Workforce Union is demanding better working conditions and higher pay. Do you negotiate, risking a decrease in your profit margins, or ignore their demands and risk a strike?

Interacting with Superiors and Subordinates

Your decisions will not go unnoticed. During the quarterly report, you will interact with key figures in the corporation:

  • Your Superior (The CEO/Board of Directors): You will need to justify your decisions and present a clear vision for the future. Your performance will be judged not just on profits, but on your ability to lead and inspire confidence.
  • Your Divisional CEOs: You will need to delegate tasks, set performance targets, and hold your subordinates accountable. Your ability to manage your team will be crucial to your success.

The Annual Shareholder Meeting: The Ultimate Test

At the end of each year, you will face the ultimate test of your leadership: the Annual Shareholder Meeting. Here, you will present your company's performance to the various shareholder factions, each with their own agenda. You will need to answer tough questions, defend your decisions, and secure the support of the shareholders to continue in your role. Your reputation, your career, and the future of the company are on the line.

The Criminal Underworld & Covert Operations

Beyond the legitimate markets and political maneuvering lies a shadowy network of criminal elements. Engaging with this underworld can unlock powerful, albeit risky, avenues for corporate advancement, allowing you to bend rules, disrupt rivals, and secure illicit gains.

Agents, Thugs, and Schemes

The criminal underworld is populated by a diverse cast of characters, from cunning corporate spies to brutal enforcers. You can recruit these individuals or leverage their services to execute a variety of covert schemes.

  • Recruit Criminal Characters: Hire specialists like data brokers, saboteurs, or enforcers, each with unique skills and loyalty levels.
  • Execute Covert Schemes: Direct agents to steal rival research, disrupt competitor supply lines, or even assassinate key personnel.
  • Personal Involvement: As the Chief Corporate Officer, you can personally undertake high-stakes covert missions, risking your reputation and freedom for significant rewards.
  • Internal Agents: Designate trusted employees within your own HR department to act as internal agents, leveraging their access for corporate espionage or counter-intelligence.

Illicit Contracts & Black Markets

The underworld operates on its own set of rules, offering black market opportunities and the ability to issue 'criminal contracts' for others to fulfill, further blurring the lines between legitimate business and illicit enterprise.

  • Create Criminal Contracts: Post bounties or offer rewards for specific illicit actions, such as resource theft, data breaches, or corporate sabotage, allowing other criminal factions to bid on and execute them.
  • Black Market Access: Buy and sell restricted resources, stolen goods, or advanced prototypes that are unavailable through legitimate channels.
  • Reputation & Risk: Engaging with the underworld carries significant risks, including exposure, legal repercussions, and damage to your public image. However, successful operations can yield immense profits and strategic advantages.
  • Underworld Factions: Navigate relationships with various criminal syndicates, each with their own agendas, territories, and preferred methods of operation.

Characters and Human Resources

Your corporation is not just a collection of buildings and ships; it is a living organization made up of individuals with their own ambitions, strengths, and weaknesses. Managing your human resources is just as important as managing your production lines.

Employee Management

You will be responsible for hiring, promoting, and managing your employees. Each employee will have a unique set of skills, traits, and a personality that will affect their performance.

  • Hiring: Choose from a pool of candidates, each with their own resume and background.
  • Promotions: Recognize and reward your top performers by promoting them to positions of greater responsibility. A line worker today could be a divisional CEO tomorrow.
  • Training and Development: Invest in your employees to improve their skills and unlock new abilities.
  • Morale and Satisfaction: Keep your employees happy and motivated to maximize their productivity.

Character Dynamics

Your interactions with your employees will have a significant impact on the game. Your decisions can lead to a variety of outcomes, both positive and negative.

  • Loyalty and Betrayal: Your employees will remember how you treat them. A loyal employee may go above and beyond for you, while a disgruntled employee may leak sensitive information to a rival corporation.
  • Relationships: Your employees will form relationships with each other, creating a web of alliances and rivalries within the corporation.
  • Emergent Stories: The character system is designed to create emergent narratives. You may find yourself dealing with a brilliant but insubordinate scientist, a charismatic but corrupt CEO, or a loyal and dependable logistics manager who becomes your most trusted advisor.

Legacy and Meta-Progression

Your actions will have a lasting impact on the game world. Each playthrough will contribute to a persistent legacy, and you will be able to track your progress and achievements across multiple games.

Corporate Legacy

Your corporation will leave its mark on the solar system. Your achievements will be recorded and will affect future playthroughs.

  • Milestones and Quotas: Your performance will be tracked against a variety of metrics, such as total profit, number of employees, and technological advancements.
  • Accolades and Awards: You can earn a variety of accolades and awards for your achievements, such as "Corporation of the Century" or the "Nobel Prize for Physics."
  • Historical Archives: Your company's history will be recorded in a persistent archive, which you can view at any time.

Meta-Game Progression

Your journey as a corporate leader will span multiple playthroughs. Your successes and failures will unlock new content and challenges.

  • Persistent Player Profile: Your stats and achievements will be tracked across all of your playthroughs.
  • Unlockable Content: Your performance will unlock new starting scenarios, challenges, and game modes.
  • Character Persistence: Characters from your previous playthroughs, including your own, may appear in future games as employees, rivals, or even the CEO of a competing corporation.
  • New Star System, New Game: Each new game places your corporation as the first to arrive at a newly discovered star system. As the game progresses, other corporations will establish themselves, leading to a dynamic and evolving competitive landscape. This narrative explains why the game world resets each playthrough, while allowing your characters and their legacies to persist as they journey to new frontiers.
