Title IV: Commerce, Science, and Transportation

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to commerce, science, and transportation. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from the Coast Guard to NASA. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Coast Guard Mission Readiness

This section of the bill provides a significant funding increase for the Coast Guard to improve its mission readiness.

  • Special Appropriations: The bill provides $24,593,500,000 for the Coast Guard for fiscal year 2025.

Spectrum Auctions

This section of the bill authorizes the FCC to hold new auctions for the use of the electromagnetic spectrum, which could generate billions of dollars in revenue.

  • Spectrum Auctions: The bill extends the FCC's authority to conduct spectrum auctions until September 30, 2034.

Air Traffic Control Improvements

This section of the bill invests in modernizing the air traffic control system.

  • Air Traffic Control Improvements: The bill provides significant funding for the modernization of the air traffic control system, including funding for new radar systems, runway safety technologies, and a new air route traffic control center.

Space Launch and Reentry Licensing and Permitting User Fees

This section of the bill introduces user fees for commercial space launches.

  • User Fees: The bill requires the Secretary of Transportation to impose a fee on each commercial space launch and reentry.

Mars Missions, Artemis Missions, and Moon to Mars Program

This section of the bill provides funding for NASA's Mars and Artemis missions.

  • Special Appropriations: The bill provides $9,995,000,000 for NASA's Mars missions, Artemis missions, and Moon to Mars program.

Corporate Average Fuel Economy Civil Penalties

This section of the bill repeals penalties for airlines that fail to meet fuel economy standards.

  • Repeal of Penalties: The bill repeals the civil penalties for airlines that fail to meet corporate average fuel economy standards.

Payments for Lease of Metropolitan Washington Airports

This section of the bill makes changes to the lease payments for the Metropolitan Washington Airports.

  • Lease Payments: The bill increases the annual lease payment for the Metropolitan Washington Airports to $15,000,000.

Rescission of Certain Amounts for the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration

This section of the bill rescinds funding for the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

  • Rescission of Funds: The bill rescinds unobligated balances of amounts made available to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

Reduction in Annual Transfers to Travel Promotion Fund

This section of the bill reduces the annual transfer to the Travel Promotion Fund.

  • Reduction in Transfers: The bill reduces the annual transfer to the Travel Promotion Fund from $100,000,000 to $20,000,000.

Treatment of Unobligated Funds for Alternative Fuel and Low-Emission Aviation Technology

This section of the bill rescinds funding for alternative fuel and low-emission aviation technology.

  • Rescission of Funds: The bill rescinds unobligated balances of amounts made available for alternative fuel and low-emission aviation technology.

Rescission of Amounts Appropriated to Public Wireless Supply Chain Innovation Fund

This section of the bill rescinds funding for the Public Wireless Supply Chain Innovation Fund.

  • Rescission of Funds: The bill rescinds $850,000,000 from the Public Wireless Supply Chain Innovation Fund.
