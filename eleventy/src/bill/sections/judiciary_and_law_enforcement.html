Title X: Judiciary

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to the judiciary and law enforcement. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from immigration fees to the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Subtitle A: Immigration and Law Enforcement Matters

This subtitle focuses on changes to immigration and law enforcement programs.

  • Immigration Fees: The bill introduces a wide range of new fees for immigration applications, including for asylum, employment authorization, and adjustment of status.
  • Immigration and Law Enforcement Funding: The bill provides additional funding for the Department of Homeland Security and the Department of Justice to carry out immigration and law enforcement activities.

Subtitle B: Judiciary Matters

This subtitle focuses on changes to judiciary matters.

  • Appropriation to the Administrative Office of the United States Courts: The bill provides funding to the Administrative Office of the United States Courts to examine the state of the dockets of the courts and to prepare and transmit statistical data and reports as to the business of the courts.
  • Appropriation to the Federal Judicial Center: The bill provides funding to the Federal Judicial Center to stimulate, create, develop, and conduct programs of continuing education and training for personnel of the judicial branch.

Subtitle C: Radiation Exposure Compensation Matters

This subtitle focuses on changes to the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act.

  • Extension of Fund: The bill extends the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act until December 31, 2028.
  • Claims Relating to Atmospheric Testing: The bill makes changes to the claims process for individuals who were exposed to radiation from atmospheric nuclear testing.
  • Claims Relating to Uranium Mining: The bill makes changes to the claims process for individuals who were exposed to radiation from uranium mining.
  • Claims Relating to Manhattan Project Waste: The bill creates a new claims process for individuals who were exposed to radiation from Manhattan Project waste.
  • Limitations on Claims: The bill makes changes to the limitations on claims under the Radiation Exposure Compensation Act.
