Title III: Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to banking, housing, and urban affairs. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau to the Securities and Exchange Commission. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Funding Cap for the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection

This section of the bill caps the funding for the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection, which could limit its ability to regulate the financial industry.

  • Funding Cap: The bill caps the funding for the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection at 6.5 percent of the total operating expenses of the Federal Reserve System.

Rescission of Funds for Green and Resilient Retrofit Program for Multifamily Housing

This section of the bill rescinds funding for a program that provides green and resilient retrofits for multifamily housing.

  • Rescission of Funds: The bill rescinds all unobligated balances of amounts made available for the Green and Resilient Retrofit Program for Multifamily Housing.

Securities and Exchange Commission Reserve Fund

This section of the bill makes changes to the Securities and Exchange Commission's reserve fund, which could affect its operations.

  • SEC Reserve Fund: The bill eliminates the Securities and Exchange Commission Reserve Fund and transfers any remaining balances to the general fund of the Treasury.

Appropriations for Defense Production Act

This section of the bill appropriates $1 billion to carry out the Defense Production Act.

  • Appropriations: The bill appropriates $1,000,000,000 for fiscal year 2025 to carry out the Defense Production Act.
