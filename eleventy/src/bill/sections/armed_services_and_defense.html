Title II: Armed Services

This title of H.R. 1 authorizes a massive increase in funding for the Department of Defense, with a focus on shipbuilding, missile defense, and munitions production. The bill also includes provisions to improve the quality of life for military personnel, with increased funding for housing, pay, and benefits. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Enhancement of Department of Defense Resources

This section of the bill authorizes a very large increase in defense spending for fiscal year 2025.

  • Improving the Quality of Life for Military Personnel: The bill provides significant funding to improve the quality of life for military personnel, including funding for housing, pay, and benefits.
  • Shipbuilding: A major focus of the bill is on expanding the U.S. Navy, with billions of dollars allocated for the construction of new destroyers, submarines, and other vessels.
  • Integrated Air and Missile Defense: The bill invests heavily in next-generation missile defense technologies, including hypersonic and space-based systems.
  • Munitions and Defense Supply Chain Resiliency: The bill aims to increase the production of a wide range of munitions, from small arms ammunition to long-range missiles.
  • Scaling Low-Cost Weapons into Production: The bill provides funding to scale up the production of low-cost weapons.
  • Improving the Efficiency and Cybersecurity of the Department of Defense: The bill provides funding to improve the efficiency and cybersecurity of the Department of Defense.
  • Air Superiority: The bill provides funding to enhance the air superiority of the United States.
  • Nuclear Forces: The bill provides funding to enhance the nuclear forces of the United States.
  • Improving Capabilities of United States Indo-Pacific Command: The bill provides funding to improve the capabilities of the United States Indo-Pacific Command.
  • Improving the Readiness of the Department of Defense: The bill provides funding to improve the readiness of the Department of Defense.
  • Improving Department of Defense Border Support and Counter-Drug Missions: The bill provides funding to improve the Department of Defense's border support and counter-drug missions.
  • Department of Defense Oversight: The bill provides funding for the Department of Defense's Inspector General to conduct oversight of the department's programs and operations.
  • Military Construction Projects Authorized: The bill authorizes a number of military construction projects.
