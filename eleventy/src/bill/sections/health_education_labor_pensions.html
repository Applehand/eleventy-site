Title VIII: Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to health, education, labor, and pensions. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from student loans to the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Subtitle A: Exemption of Certain Assets

This subtitle exempts certain assets from being counted when determining a student's eligibility for federal financial aid.

  • Exemption of Certain Assets: The bill exempts family farms, small businesses, and commercial fishing businesses from being counted as assets when determining a student's eligibility for federal financial aid.

Subtitle B: Loan Limits

This subtitle makes changes to the federal student loan program, including increasing the loan limits for graduate and professional students and terminating the Graduate and Professional PLUS Loan program.

  • Establishment of Loan Limits for Graduate and Professional Students and Parent Borrowers; Termination of Graduate and Professional PLUS Loans: The bill increases the annual and aggregate loan limits for graduate and professional students and for parent borrowers. It also terminates the Graduate and Professional PLUS Loan program.

Subtitle C: Loan Repayment

This subtitle makes changes to the federal student loan repayment system, including creating a new income-based repayment plan and eliminating the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program for new borrowers.

  • Loan Repayment: The bill creates a new income-based repayment plan called the "Repayment Assistance Plan." It also eliminates the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program for new borrowers.
  • Deferment; Forbearance: The bill makes changes to the deferment and forbearance options available to student loan borrowers.
  • Loan Rehabilitation: The bill makes changes to the loan rehabilitation program, which allows borrowers to bring their defaulted loans back into good standing.
  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness: The bill eliminates the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program for new borrowers.
  • Student Loan Servicing: The bill provides additional funding for student loan servicing.

Subtitle D: Pell Grants

This subtitle makes changes to the Pell Grant program, including creating a new "Workforce Pell Grant" for short-term job training programs.

  • Eligibility: The bill makes changes to the eligibility requirements for the Pell Grant program.
  • Workforce Pell Grants: The bill creates a new "Workforce Pell Grant" program to provide grants to students enrolled in short-term job training programs.
  • Pell Shortfall: The bill provides additional funding to address the Pell Grant shortfall.
  • Federal Pell Grant Exclusion Relating to Other Grant Aid: The bill excludes Federal Pell Grants from being considered as other grant aid when determining a student's eligibility for other federal financial aid.

Subtitle E: Accountability

This subtitle makes changes to the accountability requirements for institutions of higher education.

  • Ineligibility Based on Low Earning Outcomes: The bill makes institutions of higher education ineligible to participate in federal student aid programs if their graduates have low earnings.

Subtitle F: Regulatory Relief

This subtitle provides regulatory relief to institutions of higher education.

  • Delay of Rule Relating to Borrower Defense to Repayment: The bill delays the implementation of the borrower defense to repayment rule.
  • Delay of Rule Relating to Closed School Discharges: The bill delays the implementation of the closed school discharge rule.

Subtitle G: Garden of Heroes

This subtitle provides funding for the Garden of Heroes.

  • Garden of Heroes: The bill provides $40,000,000 for the procurement of statues for the National Garden of American Heroes.

Subtitle H: Office of Refugee Resettlement

This subtitle provides funding for the Office of Refugee Resettlement.

  • Potential Sponsor Vetting for Unaccompanied Alien Children Appropriation: The bill provides $300,000,000 for the Office of Refugee Resettlement to conduct background checks on potential sponsors of unaccompanied alien children.
