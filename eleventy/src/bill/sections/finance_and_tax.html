Title VII: Finance

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to the tax code. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from individual income taxes to business taxes. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Subtitle A: Tax

This subtitle focuses on changes to the tax code.

  • Providing Permanent Tax Relief for Middle-Class Families and Workers: This chapter of the bill extends and enhances a number of tax cuts for individuals, including the reduced individual income tax rates, the increased standard deduction, and the child tax credit.
  • Delivering on Presidential Priorities to Provide New Middle-Class Tax Relief: This chapter of the bill introduces a number of new tax cuts for individuals, including a deduction for tips, a deduction for overtime pay, and a deduction for car loan interest.
  • Establishing Certainty and Competitiveness for American Job Creators: This chapter of the bill makes a number of changes to business taxes, including making full expensing permanent, increasing the research and development tax credit, and repealing the corporate alternative minimum tax.
  • Investing in American Families, Communities, and Small Businesses: This chapter of the bill provides a number of tax breaks for families and small businesses, including an enhanced child and dependent care tax credit, an expansion of the adoption tax credit, and a new tax credit for contributions to scholarship granting organizations.
  • Ending Green New Deal Spending, Promoting America-First Energy, and Other Reforms: This chapter of the bill repeals a number of clean energy tax credits, including the electric vehicle tax credit, the clean energy production tax credit, and the clean energy investment tax credit.
  • Enhancing Deduction and Income Tax Credit Guardrails, and Other Reforms: This chapter of the bill makes a number of changes to tax deductions and credits, including a limitation on the deduction for state and local taxes, a modification of the earned income tax credit, and a new excise tax on certain remittance transfers.

Subtitle B: Health

This subtitle makes significant changes to the Affordable Care Act and other health care programs.

  • Medicaid: This chapter of the bill makes a number of changes to the Medicaid program, including imposing new work requirements for able-bodied adults, reducing federal funding for the program, and giving states more flexibility to change their Medicaid programs.
  • Medicare: This chapter of the bill makes a number of changes to the Medicare program, including increasing the Medicare physician fee schedule and expanding the use of orphan drugs.
  • Health Tax: This chapter of the bill makes a number of changes to the health tax provisions of the Affordable Care Act, including repealing the individual mandate and the employer mandate.
  • Protecting Rural Hospitals and Providers: This chapter of the bill provides funding to protect rural hospitals and providers.

Subtitle C: Increase in Debt Limit

This subtitle increases the debt limit.

  • Modification of Limitation on the Public Debt: The bill increases the public debt limit by $5,000,000,000,000.

Subtitle D: Unemployment

This subtitle makes changes to the unemployment insurance system.

  • Ending Unemployment Payments to Jobless Millionaires: The bill ends unemployment payments to individuals with an adjusted gross income of over $1,000,000.
