Title I: Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to agriculture, nutrition, and forestry. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) to crop insurance and conservation programs. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Subtitle A: Nutrition

This subtitle focuses on changes to the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), formerly known as the food stamp program.

  • Re-evaluation of Thrifty Food Plan: The bill requires a re-evaluation of the Thrifty Food Plan, which is the basis for determining SNAP benefit amounts. This could result in a significant change in the amount of food assistance that low-income families receive. The bill states that the re-evaluation will be based on "current food prices, food composition data, consumption patterns, and dietary guidance."
  • Modifications to SNAP Work Requirements: The bill tightens work requirements for able-bodied adults without dependents. It also expands the definition of who is considered an able-bodied adult.
  • Restrictions on Internet Expenses: The bill prohibits the use of SNAP benefits to pay for internet service. This could make it more difficult for low-income families to access online resources for job searching, education, and other essential services.

Subtitle B: Forestry

This subtitle rescinds funding for a number of forestry programs, which could have a significant impact on the management of national forests and the prevention of wildfires.

  • Rescission of Amounts for Forestry: The bill rescinds unobligated funds for a number of forestry programs, including those related to hazardous fuels reduction, forest restoration, and wildfire prevention.

Subtitle C: Commodities

This subtitle makes a number of changes to agricultural commodity programs, including those that provide price supports and risk management tools for farmers.

  • Effective Reference Price: The bill increases the effective reference price for a number of commodities, which could result in higher subsidy payments to farmers.
  • Base Acres: The bill makes changes to the way that base acres are calculated, which could affect the amount of subsidies that farmers receive.

Subtitle D: Disaster Assistance Programs

This subtitle makes changes to a number of disaster assistance programs for farmers and ranchers.

  • Supplemental Agricultural Disaster Assistance: The bill makes changes to the Supplemental Agricultural Disaster Assistance program, which provides assistance to farmers and ranchers who have been affected by natural disasters.

Subtitle E: Crop Insurance

This subtitle makes a number of changes to the federal crop insurance program, which is a key risk management tool for farmers.

  • Beginning Farmer and Rancher Benefit: The bill increases the benefits available to beginning farmers and ranchers under the crop insurance program.
  • Area-Based Crop Insurance: The bill makes changes to area-based crop insurance plans, which are designed to provide coverage for widespread losses.

Subtitle F: Additional Investments in Rural America

This subtitle provides additional funding for a number of programs that support rural communities.

  • Conservation: The bill provides additional funding for conservation programs, which help farmers and ranchers to protect natural resources.
  • Supplemental Agricultural Trade Promotion Program: The bill provides funding for a program that promotes the export of U.S. agricultural products.
