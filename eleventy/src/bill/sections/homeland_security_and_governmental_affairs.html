Title IX: Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs

This title of H.R. 1 makes significant changes to programs related to homeland security and governmental affairs. The changes are far-reaching, affecting everything from border security to the federal workforce. Below is a detailed breakdown of the key provisions in this title.

Subtitle A: Homeland Security Provisions

This subtitle focuses on changes to homeland security programs.

  • Border Infrastructure and Wall System: The bill provides $46,550,000,000 for the construction of a wall system on the U.S.-Mexico border.
  • U.S. Customs and Border Protection Personnel, Fleet Vehicles, and Facilities: The bill provides funding to hire and train additional Border Patrol agents, and to purchase new vehicles and facilities.
  • Detention Capacity: The bill provides funding to expand detention capacity for undocumented immigrants.
  • Border Security, Technology, and Screening: The bill provides funding for new border security technologies, including surveillance towers, drones, and sensors.
  • State and Local Assistance: The bill provides funding to states and local governments to assist with border security.
  • Presidential Residence Protection: The bill provides funding for the protection of the President's residences.
  • Department of Homeland Security Appropriations for Border Support: The bill provides additional funding to the Department of Homeland Security for border support.

Subtitle B: Governmental Affairs Provisions

This subtitle focuses on changes to governmental affairs programs.

  • FEHB Improvements: The bill makes a number of changes to the Federal Employees Health Benefits program.
  • Pandemic Response Accountability Committee: The bill provides funding for the Pandemic Response Accountability Committee.
  • Appropriation for the Office of Management and Budget: The bill provides funding for the Office of Management and Budget.
